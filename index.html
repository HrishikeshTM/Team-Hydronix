About The Project

Exploring the science behind air lubrication systems and their application in marine drag reduction

Project Overview

This project, titled "Drag Reduction Using Air Lubrication System", was undertaken as part of the Class 12 Physics curriculum with the aim of applying classroom concepts to a practical and innovation-driven experiment.

The project explores how introducing a layer of air beneath a moving body in water can help reduce resistance and enhance motion efficiency. As a team, we designed and built a working model to simulate the functioning of an Air Lubrication System (ALS).

By conducting comparative tests - with and without air lubrication - we were able to measure and analyze the system's impact on performance. Through these experiments, we observed a noticeable improvement in acceleration and calculated the relative increase in efficiency.

Conclusion & Efficiency

Key findings and efficiency improvements from our research

Project Success

This project successfully demonstrated the working of an air lubrication system on a 50 cm 3D-printed boat. By injecting air bubbles beneath the hull, we created a layer of air between the water and the boat's surface, resulting in smoother movement and better overall performance.

During testing, the system consistently improved the boat's speed and reduced motor load, leading to a total efficiency gain of:

6%

The model operated as intended, and all components worked in coordination to achieve the desired effect. This confirmed that introducing air into the water around the hull can positively impact the movement and energy usage of small watercraft.
